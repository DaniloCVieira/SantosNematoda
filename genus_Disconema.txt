  <h3>
    <strong>Marine Nematode Genera from Santos Basin – an illustrative guide to the morphological variety</strong>
  </h3>
  <p>
    This
    guide
    aims
    to
    illustrate
    the
    morphological
    variety
    found
    within
    the
    marine
    Nematoda
    genus
    registered
    during
    the
    Santos
    Basin
    Regional
    Environmental
    Characterization
    Project
    (PCR-BS),
    coordinated
    by
    PETROBRAS.
    The
    potential
    of
    this
    guide
    in
    aiding
    future
    research
    on
    marine
    nematodes
    is
    notorious.
    At
    present,
    the
    species
    are
    not
    formally
    described,
    however,
    there
    is
    great
    potential
    for
    new
    species
    and
    genera,
    for
    which
    we
    will
    be
    glad
    to
    have
    collaborators
    in
    the
    descriptions.
    Most
    families
    and
    genera
    diagnoses
    were
    based
    on
    Schmidt-Rhaesa
    et
    al.
    (2014)
    and
    the
    chapters
    therein,
    except
    if
    more
    recent
    reviews
    were
    available.
  </p>
  <p>
    The
    Santos
    Basin
    is
    one
    of
    the
    main
    petroliferous
    basins
    in
    the
    Brazilian
    continental
    margin,
    currently
    responsible
    for
    74%
    of
    Brazil’s
    oil
    and
    gas
    production.
    It
    is
    located
    between
    the
    Cabo
    Frio
    High
    (22°S),
    in
    Rio
    de
    Janeiro
    state
    (RJ),
    and
    the
    Florianopolis
    High
    (28.5°S),
    in
    Santa
    Catarina
    state
    (SC),
    in
    the
    southeastern
    region
    of
    the
    Brazilian
    margin.
    The
    basin
    occupies
    an
    area
    of
    approximately
    350,000
    km2,
    bordering
    several
    Brazilian
    states
    and
    reaching
    down
    to
    2500
    m
    water
    depth
    in
    the
    São
    Paulo
    Plateau
    (Sousa
    et
    al.
    2023).
  </p>
  <p>
    The
    environmental
    characterization
    project
    included
    benthic
    and
    pelagic
    cruises
    carried
    out
    between
    2019
    and
    2022.
    A
    total
    of
    100
    sampling
    stations
    were
    distributed
    in
    eight
    transects
    perpendicular
    to
    the
    coast
    and
    samples
    were
    taken
    at
    11
    isobaths
    (25
    to
    2400
    m).
    Meiofauna
    and
    other
    biological
    and
    environmental
    variables
    were
    collected
    from
    each
    box-core
    or
    van
    Veen
    grabs
    and
    analyzed
    by
    several
    research
    parties.
    The
    distribution
    of
    meiofauna
    indicators
    in
    relation
    to
    environmental
    variables
    was
    modeled,
    using
    machine
    learning
    techniques,
    to
    provide
    baseline
    information
    in
    predicting
    large-scale
    spatial
    patterns
    and
    fostering
    future
    monitoring
    programs
    (Gallucci
    et
    al.,
    2023).
  </p>
